Frequently Asked Questions About National Parks:

What is the most famous National Park in the world?
The Grand Canyon, in Grand Canyon National Park in Arizona, is known as one of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World.

What is the most visited National Park in the world?
Great Smoky Mountains National Park is the most visited national park with more than 9 million guests per year.

Who is the director of the National Park Service?
Margaret Everson, Counselor to the Secretary, is exercising the delegated authority of the Director of the National Park Service.

What government agency oversees the National Park Service?
The National Park Service is a bureau of the Department of the Interior.

How old is the National Park System?
The National Park Service was created by an act signed by President Woodrow Wilson on August 25, 1916.

What is the origin of the National Park Service arrowhead?
The arrowhead was authorized as the official National Park Service emblem by the Secretary of the Interior on July 20, 1951.

How many areas are in the National Park System?
The system includes 419 areas covering more than 85 million acres in every state, the District of Columbia, American Samoa, Guam, Puerto Rico, and the Virgin Islands.

What is the smallest national park site?
Smallest: Thaddeus Kosciuszko National Memorial, PA, at 0.02 acres.

What is the largest national park site?
Largest: Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve, AK, at 13.2 million acres.

What is the oldest National park in the world?
Yellowstone National Park (in Wyoming, Idaho, and Montana) is the oldest U.S. national park, founded in 1872.
